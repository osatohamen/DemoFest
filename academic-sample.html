Building Effective Study Habits

  • Circe's Campus
  • Academic

Need help managing your academic standing? Falling behind on classes? Almost every student goes through a cycle of demotivation, and sometimes it’s hard getting back on track. Our university offers many supports to protect your GPA and resources that can help you stay on top of your studies while maintaining your social life.

The university offers many interactive opportunities to aid students struggling with a class, the most prevalent one being workshops. There are several workshops offered on almost a weekly basis that are led by a specialist who can help you in a more interactive way to grasp topics and work through some practice problems to really strengthen your knowledge on a topic you may have a doubt about.

In addition, we also have Math and Science Study Halls offered on a daily basis, where experts are able to help you one-on-one with any doubts you may have. Through the student life portal, you can also book an appointment with an upper-year who will be able to help you further with any doubts on any content. Our university also offers math help through a tool called Nool, which provides videos and worked out examples on how to solve problems.

There are also many resources available online! You can always check out Youtube videos on a topic you may be struggling with, or even blogs or reaching out to a fellow peer. Students have made several Discord servers to help with the opportunity to ask questions with no judgement involved.

Most importantly, you can always take advantage of office hours to reach out to a professor themselves who will be able to help you! It’s their job, so there’s no shame in asking for help! They want to see you succeed, and with the proper guidance and assistance from them, they’ll be able to navigate you back on the right path so you can accomplish your academic goals.

For any more help, you’re always able to reach out or leave a comment on our forum on particular questions or concerns. There’s always options and routes to take to tackle academic issues just with a bit of organization, guidance and support from your peers or professionals.
